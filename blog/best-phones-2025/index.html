Best Smartphones of 2025: Our Top Picks Across Every Budget

Tech Editor June 10, 2025 16 min read
SamsungAppleXiaomiBuying Guide

We spent weeks testing chipsets, cameras, battery life, and software support across more than 20 devices. This guide lists the best smartphones of 2025 in every price range, from budget phones under $300 to full flagships, with honest advice on who each one is actually for.

Buying a new phone in 2025 is harder than it used to be. Prices have climbed, marketing language has gotten louder, and the gap between a $250 phone and a $1,200 phone is no longer obvious from the spec sheet alone. Two phones can list the same screen size and a similar camera megapixel count, yet feel completely different to use every day.

This guide cuts through that. Instead of ranking phones by hype, we group them by what actually matters: who each phone suits, what it does well, and where it falls short. Whether you have $150 to spend or $1,300, you will find a clear recommendation below, along with the reasoning behind it.

How We Tested and Ranked These Phones

Every phone on this list was evaluated against the same five criteria, weighted differently depending on price bracket:

  • Everyday performance — how the phone handles apps, multitasking, and typical daily use, not just benchmark scores.
  • Camera quality — real photo and video output in daylight, indoor lighting, and low light, not just megapixel count.
  • Battery life and charging speed — screen-on time under normal use, plus how long a full charge actually takes.
  • Display quality — brightness, color accuracy, and refresh rate smoothness.
  • Software support — how many years of OS updates and security patches the manufacturer guarantees.

A $150 phone was never expected to beat a $1,300 flagship on raw performance. Instead, we asked whether each phone delivers strong value for its specific price bracket. That is the only fair way to compare a budget phone against a flagship.

Best Overall Flagship Phones of 2025

Flagship phones in 2025 all cost close to $1,000 or more, so the question is not which one is "best" in the abstract. It is which one fits how you actually use your phone. We tested three that stand out clearly above the rest.

Samsung Galaxy S25 Ultra — Best for Power Users

The Galaxy S25 Ultra remains the phone to beat if you want everything in one device. It pairs a 200MP main camera with a Snapdragon 8 Elite chipset, a built-in S Pen, and a titanium frame that survives daily drops better than glass-backed rivals. Samsung backs it with seven years of OS and security updates, which is the longest commitment in the industry alongside Google.

The trade-off is size and price. At 218 grams and $1,299, it is not a phone you forget you are carrying, and wireless charging tops out at a modest 15W. If you want the most capable Android phone money can buy and don't mind the bulk, this is it. You can check the full spec sheet on our Galaxy S25 Ultra page, and Samsung's own product page has additional trade-in details at samsung.com.

Apple iPhone 16 Pro Max — Best for Video and Longevity

Apple's iPhone 16 Pro Max is the strongest choice for anyone who records a lot of video. The A18 Pro chip enables 4K 120fps ProRes capture that used to require a dedicated camera rig, and the new Camera Control button lets you adjust exposure and zoom without touching the screen. Apple's five to six years of realistic software support (often longer in practice) also means this phone will still get major updates well into the late 2020s.

At $1,199 it costs slightly less than the Galaxy S25 Ultra, but you lose the stylus and get 60Hz on nothing — ProMotion only applies to the display refresh, not charging speed, which sits at a conservative 27W wired. If your workflow already lives inside iOS and Final Cut, this is the obvious pick. Full specs are on our iPhone 16 Pro Max page, with additional details at apple.com.

Xiaomi 15 Pro — Best Flagship Value

The Xiaomi 15 Pro matches Snapdragon 8 Elite performance from the Galaxy S25 Ultra at a full $400 less. Its standout feature is the 6,100mAh battery, the largest of any phone in this comparison, paired with 90W wired charging that refills it in under 45 minutes. Leica-tuned cameras handle color science well, especially in daylight.

The catch is software polish outside China. Google service integration can feel less refined than on a Pixel or Galaxy device in some regions, and global availability varies by country. If battery anxiety is your main complaint with flagship phones, this is the one to look at. See the full breakdown on our Xiaomi 15 Pro page.

Editor's Note If you upgrade every two to three years, the software update window matters less. Buy for the features you will use daily, not the number on the update roadmap.

Best Mid-Range Smartphones of 2025

The mid-range segment, roughly $400 to $700, has improved more than any other price bracket over the past two years. Chipsets like the Snapdragon 7 Gen 1 now handle everyday tasks, social apps, and light gaming with barely any lag compared to a flagship.

Samsung Galaxy A55 — Best Mid-Range All-Rounder

The Galaxy A55 is proof that you no longer need to spend $1,000 to get a durable, well-supported phone. It has IP67 water resistance, a 50MP main camera with optical image stabilization, and five years of guaranteed software updates — an update commitment that used to be flagship-exclusive. At $449, it undercuts most rivals in its category while matching or beating them on build quality.

Performance is the compromise. The Exynos 1480 chipset handles daily use comfortably but will show its limits in demanding games at max settings. For most buyers who want a phone that lasts five years without falling apart, this is the safest mid-range pick.

Realme GT 6 — Best for Fast Charging

The Realme GT 6 targets a different kind of buyer: someone who wants near-flagship speed without near-flagship pricing. Its Snapdragon 8s Gen 3 chipset and 120W charging (0 to 100% in about 20 minutes) make it one of the fastest-charging phones at any price. The Sony IMX906 main sensor also punches above its weight for photography.

At $599, it sits between typical mid-range and flagship pricing, and that positioning is deliberate — Realme is chasing gamers and power users who prioritize speed over camera versatility. If you hate waiting for your phone to charge, this is worth a look.

Best Budget Smartphones Under $300

Budget phones improved the most out of any category in 2025. A phone under $300 today often outperforms a $500 phone from three years ago, particularly on display quality and 5G connectivity.

Infinix GT 20 Pro — Best Budget Overall

The Infinix GT 20 Pro is the clearest budget recommendation on this list. Its 144Hz AMOLED display is normally reserved for phones costing twice as much, and the Dimensity 8200 chipset handles casual and mid-tier gaming smoothly. At $299, it is difficult to find a phone this well-rounded for less.

Nokia G42 5G — Best for Durability and Repair

The Nokia G42 5G takes a different approach. Instead of chasing spec-sheet wins, it earned iFixit's highest repairability score of any recent phone, meaning you can replace the battery or screen yourself with basic tools. Paired with three years of security updates and a $199 price tag, it is the phone we recommend for buyers who keep devices for a long time and want to avoid landfill-bound electronics.

itel S25 Pro — Best Under $150

The itel S25 Pro is aimed squarely at first-time smartphone buyers or anyone replacing a broken device on a strict budget. At $149, it will not compete on camera quality or gaming performance, but it covers calls, messaging, social media, and light browsing reliably, which is what most entry-level buyers actually need.

Best Camera Phones of 2025

Camera performance is the spec people ask about most, and it is also the hardest to judge from a number on a box. Megapixel count barely correlates with real photo quality once you go past 50MP — sensor size, processing software, and lens quality matter far more.

Best Battery Life and Fastest Charging

Battery capacity alone does not tell the full story, since chipset efficiency varies a lot between brands. That said, capacity combined with charging speed gives a reliable practical picture.

  • Xiaomi 15 Pro — 6,100mAh with 90W wired charging (0-100% in around 45 minutes)
  • Realme GT 6 — 5,500mAh with 120W wired charging (0-100% in around 20 minutes)
  • Samsung Galaxy A55 — 5,000mAh with efficient Exynos power management for two-day light use
  • Nokia G42 5G — 5,000mAh, slower 20W charging but excellent standby efficiency

If fast charging matters more to you than raw capacity, the Realme GT 6 is the clear winner. If you would rather charge less often overall, the Xiaomi 15 Pro's larger cell wins on total uptime.

Best Smartphones for Africa and Emerging Markets

Phones sold across African markets need to handle a few things Western reviews often ignore: dual-SIM support for juggling data and voice plans, dependable battery life where charging access is inconsistent, and pricing that matches local purchasing power. The Tecno Phantom X2 Pro stands out here, bringing a periscope telephoto lens — a feature normally reserved for $1,000-plus phones — down to a much more accessible price point, with dual-SIM support built in.

The Infinix GT 20 Pro and Nokia G42 5G, both already covered above, are equally strong picks for this market. For a deeper look at network coverage and which 5G bands actually work across the continent, our 5G in Africa 2025 guide breaks down carrier support country by country.

iPhone vs Android in 2025: Which Should You Choose?

This debate rarely has a universally right answer, but there is a practical shortcut. If your close contacts, family, or workplace already use iPhones, staying in that ecosystem for iMessage, FaceTime, and AirDrop saves real friction. If you value customization, want a wider range of prices to choose from, or already use Google services heavily, Android — and Samsung specifically — usually fits better.

We cover this decision in far more depth, including a feature-by-feature breakdown, in our dedicated iPhone vs Android comparison.

How to Choose the Right Smartphone for Your Budget

Chipset and Performance Explained Simply

A chipset (also called a processor or SoC) is the brain of the phone. You do not need to memorize model numbers, but a rough rule helps: Snapdragon 8-series and Apple's A-series chips are flagship-tier, Snapdragon 7-series and Dimensity 8000-series sit in the mid-range, and anything below that is built for basic tasks rather than gaming or heavy multitasking.

Understanding 5G Without the Jargon

5G is simply a faster, lower-delay version of mobile data. Not all 5G is equal — some phones only support "sub-6GHz" 5G, which is common and reliable, while a smaller number also support "mmWave" 5G, which is faster but has much shorter range and is mostly limited to dense city areas. For most buyers, sub-6GHz 5G support is all you need to check for.

Battery Capacity and Charging Speed

Battery size is measured in mAh (milliamp-hours). As a rough guide, 4,500mAh or higher should comfortably get most people through a full day. Charging speed, measured in watts (W), determines how quickly that battery refills — anything above 65W will typically charge a phone to 100% in under 40 minutes.

Software Updates and Long-Term Value

This is the most overlooked spec on any listing. A phone that stops receiving security updates becomes a growing risk to use for banking or personal data, regardless of how fast it still runs. Samsung and Google currently lead with up to seven years of updates on flagship models; most budget phones offer two to three years, which is worth factoring into the real cost per year of ownership.

Camera Specs That Actually Matter

Ignore megapixel count as the deciding factor. Instead look for optical image stabilization (OIS) on the main camera, which reduces blur significantly, and check independent sample photos rather than marketing images whenever possible.

Common Smartphone Buying Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Buying based on megapixels alone. A 200MP sensor with poor processing can produce worse photos than a well-tuned 50MP sensor.
  2. Ignoring software update policy. A cheaper phone with only one year of updates can cost more over three years than a slightly pricier phone with five years of support.
  3. Overpaying for storage you won't use. Most people never fill a 256GB phone. Check your current phone's storage usage before assuming you need more.
  4. Skipping the return window test. Always test the camera and fingerprint sensor within the first week, while a return is still possible.
  5. Assuming more RAM always means faster. Past 8GB, additional RAM has diminishing returns for most everyday use cases.

People Also Ask

What is the best smartphone to buy in 2025?
There is no single best phone for everyone. The Samsung Galaxy S25 Ultra is the best overall flagship for power users, the iPhone 16 Pro Max is the best choice for video and long-term software support, and the Infinix GT 20 Pro is the best pick if your budget is under $300.
What is the best budget smartphone under $300?
The Infinix GT 20 Pro is the strongest all-round budget phone in 2025, thanks to its 144Hz AMOLED display and Dimensity 8200 chipset. The Nokia G42 5G is a close second if repairability and long-term durability matter more to you than raw speed.
Is it better to buy a flagship or a mid-range phone in 2025?
A mid-range phone is the smarter buy for most people in 2025, since chipsets like the Snapdragon 7 Gen 1 and Dimensity 8200 handle everyday tasks, social media, and casual gaming with no visible lag. A flagship only makes sense if you need top-tier cameras, the fastest possible performance, or the longest software support window.
Which phone has the best camera in 2025?
For overall photo and video quality, the Samsung Galaxy S25 Ultra and iPhone 16 Pro Max are the strongest all-round performers. If you want camera performance without flagship pricing, the Xiaomi 15 Pro and Realme GT 6 both offer excellent results for hundreds of dollars less.
How many years of software updates should a smartphone get?
Aim for at least three years of Android version updates and four to five years of security patches. Samsung and Google now offer up to seven years on select flagship models, which is the longest currently available in the industry.
Is 5G worth paying extra for in 2025?
In most regions, yes. 5G is now standard on most mid-range and flagship phones at no significant price premium, and network coverage has expanded enough that it is worth having for future-proofing, even if your current area still relies mostly on 4G.
Do I need 12GB of RAM in a smartphone?
Most people do not. 8GB is enough for smooth everyday multitasking. 12GB or more only becomes noticeable if you regularly run demanding games at high settings or keep dozens of heavy apps open simultaneously.
What should I check before buying a used or refurbished phone?
Check the battery health percentage, confirm the phone is not reported lost or stolen using its IMEI number, and test the charging port, cameras, and fingerprint sensor in person or during the return window before finalizing the purchase.
How long should a smartphone battery last before it needs replacing?
Most lithium-ion batteries retain around 80% of their original capacity after roughly 500 full charge cycles, which usually works out to two to three years of typical daily use before a noticeable drop in battery life.
Is it worth buying a phone with a periscope zoom camera?
Only if you photograph distant subjects often, such as wildlife, sports, or travel landmarks. For everyday photography and portraits, a standard telephoto lens delivers similar quality at a lower price.

Final Verdict

If you want a single recommendation and money is not the deciding factor, the Samsung Galaxy S25 Ultra remains the most complete flagship phone of 2025. If you would rather spend less without giving up durability or long-term software support, the Samsung Galaxy A55 is the smartest mid-range purchase we tested. And if your budget is under $300, the Infinix GT 20 Pro delivers more display and performance for the money than anything else in that range.
